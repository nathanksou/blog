# Jervis Bay

Jervis Bay is one of those places that doesn't look real — blindingly white sand, crystal clear water, and marine life everywhere you look. It's about a 2.5-hour drive south of Sydney, so you'll need to rent a car. Public transport won't cut it. If you're up for it, you can camp at Green Patch inside Booderee National Park for the full experience.

## Tips

- **You need a car.** No practical public transport options.
- **Booderee National Park pass required.** Purchase a standard 2-day pass (or more) before entering.
- **Bring snorkeling gear** if you have it — the water clarity is unreal.
- **Pack food and water.** Options inside the park are limited.

## On the Way Down

- **Kiama Blow Hole** — Worth a quick stop on the drive. A natural rock formation that shoots water into the air when the swell is right.

## Huskisson

The main coastal town and heart of Jervis Bay. Good base for food and supplies.

- **Dhaba (Indian Restaurant)** — Try the egg curry.
- **Nana Thai Restaurant** — Solid Thai.
- **Papparich** — Malaysian.
- **Gelato shop** — Grab a scoop while walking the waterfront.
- **Ernest Arthur** — Café/bar on the waterfront.

## Beaches & Snorkeling

- ★ **Blenheim Beach** — Fantastic snorkeling in calm, clear water. More secluded than the main beaches. Vibrant marine life along the rocky edges.
- **Greenfield Beach** — Soft white sand and clear blue water. Decent snorkeling along the rocks, but better as a relaxed beach day or picnic spot.
- ★ **Murrays Beach** (Booderee National Park) — Top-tier snorkeling. Crystal clear water with impressive marine diversity. Also a beautiful spot to just chill.
- **Cave Beach** (Booderee National Park) — Watch for wallabies on the beach. Rugged charm, popular with surfers and campers.
- **Abrahams Bosom Reserve** — Quieter, family-friendly beach area.

## Walks & Activities

- **Woebegone Snorkeling** — Guided snorkeling experiences if you want someone to show you the best spots.
- **The Grotto Walk (Nowra)** — A short bushwalk to a beautiful sandstone grotto. Worth the detour.
- **Gosangs Tunnel** — A short walk through a historic hand-carved tunnel in the bush.

## Camping

- **Green Patch (Booderee National Park)** — The go-to campsite. Book ahead in peak season. Wake up to kangaroos and cockatoos.
