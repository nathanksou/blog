# New Zealand

New Zealand is where dramatic landscapes meet laid-back culture. Auckland is the urban gateway — multicultural food, volcanic cones, and harbor views. Queenstown is the adventure capital — skiing, hiking, wine, and some of the best burgers you'll ever eat. The South Island is where the scenery gets unreal.

## Auckland

### Food & Drink

- **Eden Noodle House** — Authentic Sichuan cuisine with spicy, mouth-numbing noodles. Small eatery, big flavors.
- **Meat Pies** — A Kiwi cultural staple. Flaky pastry, savory fillings (minced beef, lamb, gourmet options). Grab one from any bakery — you can't go wrong.

### Things to Do

- **One Tree Hill (Maungakiekie)** — A volcanic cone and former Māori pā with sweeping city views. Surrounded by Cornwall Park with great walking trails.
- **Mount Eden (Maungawhau)** — Auckland's highest natural point. Panoramic views of the city and harbors from the crater rim. A popular and easy hike.

## Queenstown

The adventure capital. Come for the scenery, stay for the food.

### Food & Drink

- ★ **Fergburger** — Legendary. Massive, mouthwatering burgers with fresh ingredients. There's always a line and it's always worth it.
- ★ **Fergbakery** — Right next door. Flaky pastries, savory pies (try the Blue Cheese & Steak or Chilli Cheese & Steak), and artisan bread.
- **Ferggelato** — Italian-style gelato to finish. Wide variety of flavors, all excellent.
- **Kuki Time** — Freshly baked cookies in creative flavors. Soft, chewy, and dangerous.
- **Yonder** — Trendy café and bar with innovative, health-conscious dishes. Good vegan and gluten-free options.

### Nearby

- **Arrowtown** — A charming historic village from the gold rush era. Well-preserved 19th-century buildings, tree-lined streets, and great walks.
- **The Winery** — Self-paced wine tasting with a wide selection of New Zealand wines. Locations in Queenstown and Arrowtown.

## South Island Highlights

### Nature & Hiking

- ★ **Milford Sound** — Often called the "eighth wonder of the world." A fjord in Fiordland National Park with towering cliffs, waterfalls, and abundant wildlife. Explore by boat, kayak, or scenic flight.
- ★ **Hooker Valley Trail** — One of New Zealand's most popular hikes in Aoraki/Mount Cook National Park. Alpine landscapes, glacial lakes, and views of the country's highest peak.
- **Lake Wanaka** — A stunning glacial lake surrounded by mountains. Boating, fishing, hiking, or just soaking in the views.
- **Salmon Shop** — Fresh, locally sourced salmon on the drive between destinations. Smoked, fresh, or sashimi-grade.

### Skiing & Snowboarding

If you're visiting in winter (June–September), Queenstown is a world-class ski destination.

- **Coronet Peak** — Near Queenstown. Diverse runs and excellent facilities.
- **The Remarkables** — Also near Queenstown. Spectacular views and varied terrain.
- **Mt Hutt** — Near Canterbury. Some of the best powder and longest season in the country.
- **Cardrona** — Family-friendly with great terrain parks.
- **Snow Center (Queenstown)** — Gear rental and advice. Good starting point.
- **Browns Ski Rental (Queenstown)** — High-quality rental gear with great service.
