# Singapore

Singapore packs an incredible amount into a tiny island — hawker stalls serving Michelin-level food, futuristic architecture, lush gardens, and neighborhoods that each feel like a different country. It's also one of the best layover destinations in the world. It's hot and humid year-round, so dress light, carry water, and plan your walking routes to pass through air-conditioned malls along the way.

## Tips

- **Getting around:** Public transport (MRT trains and buses) is cheap and efficient. Use Grab (Southeast Asia's Uber) when you're in a hurry.
- **Stay hydrated.** Buy a drink at any convenience store (water, tea, electrolytes) and carry it as you walk.
- **Plan AC breaks.** The malls aren't just for shopping — they're strategic cooling stops.
- **Local friends.** If you know any Singaporeans, lean on them. They love sharing their food spots.

## Top Recommendations

- ★ **Kok Sen Restaurant** — A beloved Cantonese-style gem on Keong Saik Road. The Big Prawn Hor Fun and Claypot Yong Tau Foo are legendary. Family-run, humble setting, huge flavor.
- ★ **Tian Tian Hainanese Chicken Rice** — The most iconic hawker stall in Singapore, located at Maxwell Food Centre. Tender chicken, fragrant rice, simple perfection. Even Anthony Bourdain praised it. Come early — lines get long.
- ★ **SEA Aquarium (Sentosa Island)** — One of the world's largest aquariums. Over 100,000 marine animals. The Open Ocean Habitat viewing panel is jaw-dropping. Great for families and marine life enthusiasts.
- ★ **ArtScience Museum** — Where art meets technology. Features TeamLab Tokyo installations — mesmerizing, interactive digital art experiences. A must for anyone into creative innovation.
- ★ **Marina Bay Sands & Gardens by the Bay** — See them at night. Marina Bay Sands' Spectra light and water show lights up the skyline, while Gardens by the Bay's Garden Rhapsody brings the Supertrees alive with synchronized lights and music. During the day, explore the shops, visit the Merlion at Merlion Park, and walk through the Gardens.

## Neighborhoods & Attractions

### Marina Bay Area
- **The Jewel at Changi Airport** — World's tallest indoor waterfall, lush gardens, and plenty of shops and restaurants. Worth visiting even if you're not flying.
- **Merlion Park** — Home to the iconic Merlion statue with waterfront views.
- **Marina Square** — Shopping mall with retail, dining, and entertainment.
- **Gardens by the Bay** — Supertrees, Floral Fantasy, Cloud Forest (mist-filled mountain), and the Flower Dome (world's largest glass greenhouse).

### Sentosa Island
- **Universal Studios Singapore** — Theme park with movie-themed rides.
- **Adventure Cove Waterpark** — Water slides and a lazy river.
- **Beaches** — Siloso, Palawan, and Tanjong Beach for a tropical afternoon.

### Chinatown
- Temples, hawker stalls, traditional shophouses, and Michelin-starred street food. The energy here is unmatched.

### Kampong Glam & Arab Street
- **Sultan Mosque** — Iconic golden-domed mosque.
- **Haji Lane** — Narrow street packed with indie boutiques, murals, and hip cafes. Great for photos and unique shopping.
- **Arab Street** — Textile shops, Middle Eastern food, and a mix of old and new.

### Little India
- Colorful markets, Hindu temples, traditional eateries, and unique shopping. A feast for the senses.

### Orchard Road
- Singapore's premier shopping strip. High-end boutiques, flagship stores, and malls for days.

### Clarke Quay
- Riverside nightlife hub. Restaurants, bars, and entertainment with a lively atmosphere.

## Museums & Gardens

- **SAM Singapore Art Museum** — Contemporary Southeast Asian art.
- **Red Dot Design Museum** — Innovative, award-winning design in a striking red building.
- **St. Andrew's Cathedral** — Stunning neo-Gothic architecture, the oldest Anglican cathedral in Singapore.
- **Esplanade – Theatres on the Bay** — Premier performing arts center (nicknamed "The Durian" for its spiky roof).
- **Singapore Botanic Gardens** — UNESCO World Heritage site with the famous National Orchid Garden.
